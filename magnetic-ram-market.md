<p><p>Magnetic RAM (MRAM) is a type of non-volatile memory that stores data using magnetic elements. There are two main types of MRAM in the market: Toggle MRAM and STT-MRAM. Toggle MRAM works by reversing the polarization of the magnetic elements to represent data, while STT-MRAM utilizes the spin-torque effect to switch the elements' spin direction. Both types have advantages such as high-speed read/write operations, low power consumption, and excellent endurance. They are increasingly adopted in various applications, including computers, mobile devices, and IoT devices, for their performance and reliability.</p></p>
