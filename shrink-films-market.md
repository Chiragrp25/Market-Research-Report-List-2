<p><p>Shrink films, also known as shrink wrap or stretch films, are a type of packaging material made from polymer plastic that shrinks tightly around products when heat is applied. Shrink films offer several advantages such as increased product visibility, protection from external elements, and improved stability during transportation and storage.</p><p>The future outlook of the shrink films market looks promising. The market is witnessing steady growth due to factors such as the rise in e-commerce activities, growth in the retail sector, and the increasing popularity of ready-to-eat and convenience foods. The availability of alternative packaging materials and stringent regulations regarding the use of plastic films could hinder the market growth to some extent. PVC films are known for their high shrinkage rate and flexibility, making them suitable for various applications. PP/BOPP films offer excellent tensile strength, clarity, and printability. PE films are widely used in food and beverage packaging, while PET films provide superior dielectric and dimensional stability. PLA films are derived from renewable sources and find application in biodegradable and compostable packaging materials.</p></p>
